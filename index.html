How to Calculate a Tip

A tip is a percentage of your bill given to the service staff as a gratuity. To calculate manually:

  • Step 1: Multiply bill × tip% ÷ 100 = Tip amount
  • Step 2: Add tip to bill = Total amount
  • Step 3: Divide total by number of people = Per person
  • Example: $60 bill × 20% = $12 tip → $72 total → $36 each (2 people)

When to Tip

  • Sit-down restaurants: Always tip 15-20%
  • Buffets: 10% for beverage service
  • Fast food: Optional, 10% if tip jar present
  • Takeout: Optional, 5-10% for complex orders
  • Bad service: Still tip, speak to manager instead

Tipping Etiquette

  • Tip is based on pre-tax amount in most countries
  • Tipping culture varies by country — not universal
  • In US, 18-20% is the standard for good service
  • Large parties often have automatic gratuity (18-20%)
  • Cash tips are preferred by many service workers
